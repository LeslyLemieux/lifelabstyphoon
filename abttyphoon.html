About Typhoon


A typhoon is something that occurs in different parts of the world. It is a cyclone,
a big and rotating storm, that causes wind, rain, and destruction depending on how
strong it is. This also causes negative impacts to the people and community.


Conditions on how a typhoon forms

• A large part of a warm, tropical ocean water with temperature exceeding 26.5oC
is the condition needed for a typhoon to form. Then the ocean water would evaporate
to the atmosphere giving us warm air due to the heat from the Sun.
• The second condition for forming typhoons would be cold and unstable air above the
water surface, so it could meet with the warm air from the evaporation.


This will start forming clouds. Then the warm air would push the cold air downwards
as it moves forward, this will create two kinds of pressures: high pressure area
(from the presence of cold and dense air) and low pressure area (from the presence
of hot and humid air). Spiraling winds around the center or something we call the eye
of the storm is produced as the meeting of the cold and warm air continues. This will
form a tropical depression. This tropical depression can develop into something stronger:
tropical depression > storm > typhoon, and in some cases, even super typhoon.




How do landforms and bodies of water affect typhoons?

• Mountains help slow down typhoons since they block the movement of the air and rain
of the typhoon. The altitude of the mountain is also helped because this would force the
typhoon to release on only one side of the mountain. In general, land reduces the intensity
of the typhoon since the main source of typhoons is water. And land.. is of course.. not water.




Why is the Philippines prone to typhoons?

• Now that we’ve discussed what a typhoon is and how it develops, let’s move on to something
more specific to our country. You might have a question along the lines of why is the Philippines
prone to typhoons? Well, just like mentioned earlier, typhoons need a large part of a warm,
ocean water. And you know, the Philippines is a tropical island— basically surrounded with water,
and has a warm climate. Everything you need for a typhoon to formulate. So that’s why.


Now what is the general path of the typhoon that enters the PAR? But first, let’s understand what
PAR is, PAR is the Philippine Area of Responsibility, which is, according to research, “This
is the smallest and innermost monitoring domain, whose boundary is closest to the Philippine
Islands.” So what IS the general path of the typhoon that enters the PAR? It is usually west-northwest.
The reason behind this is the trade winds from the intertropical convergence zone, which is
basically the region that circles Earth near the Equator where the trade winds of the Northern
and Southern Hemispheres come together.
