Summary


- A typhoon is a cyclone, a big and rotating storm, that causes wind, rain, and
destruction depending on how strong it is.

- In order for a typhoon to form, a large part of a tropical warm ocean water
exceeding 26.5oC, warm air, and cold air is needed.

- The hot air will push down the cold air continuously. Spiraling winds around the
center or something we call the eye of the storm is produced as the meeting of the
cold and warm air continues.

- Land generally slows down typhoons because the typhoon loses its source. And the
condition of the water affects the formation of typhoons.

- The Philippines are prone to typhoons due to its geographical location as a tropical
country.

- The general direction of typhoons that enter the PAR (or Philippine Area of
Responsibility) is west-northwest.

- Typhoons can have negative effects that are pretty destructive, so it is important
for us to be able to at least minimize it.
